Part II - Influences of Loan Properties on Their Borrower APRs¶

by Dorothy Nguyen¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I would like to discover what are the major loan features for predicting the borrower interest rate of loans. The main focus was on the amount of loan, monthly loan payment, and the characteristics of the borrowers such as their incomes and credit ratings.

Dataset Overview¶

  • The studied data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan provided by Prosper Marketplace, California-based pioneering company in the peer-to-peer lending industry in the U.S.
  • Since its establishment in 2005, Prosper has helped more than 1.3 million customers get access to over 21 billion USD in affordable loans (source).
  • Out of 81 columns, I selected about 15 columns that are relevant to this loan exploration project.
  • In the preliminary wrangling, I transformed some variables into their proper data types, for example, Income Range and Prosper Rating (Alpha) being converted to be ordinal types since they have intrinsic ranking order in their natures.

# Add functions for facilitating plot drawing to avoid code repetition on the exploration

def x_y_t(xaxis,yaxis,title):
    '''
   Function that return any chart title and its X-axis, Y-axis
    '''
    plt.xlabel(xaxis,size=10)
    plt.ylabel(yaxis,size=10)
    plt.title(title,size=13);
    
def plot_hist_charts(binsize,col):
    '''
   Function that return histogram for continuous variable with its correspondingly proper binsize
    '''
    bins = np.arange(col.min(), col.max()+binsize,binsize)
    plt.figure(figsize=[10, 5])
    plt.hist(col,bins=bins)
    
def plot_log_charts(log_binsize,col):
    '''
   Function that return log transformation histogram for continuous variable with its correspondingly proper binsize
    '''
    # Condition to handle the case when a variable takes zero values and can't get logarithmic transformation
    # but need adding value 1 to all the values in that variable to make log transformation legitimate.
    if col.min()==0:
        col_new=col+1
        bins = 10 ** np.arange(np.log10(col_new.min()), np.log10(col_new.max())+log_binsize, log_binsize)
    else:
        bins = 10 ** np.arange(np.log10(col.min()), np.log10(col.max())+log_binsize, log_binsize)
    plt.figure(figsize=[10, 5])
    plt.hist(col,bins=bins)
    plt.xscale('log')

Borrower APR vs Loan Original Amount across Prosper Rating¶

  • Prosper rating scale appears to be the factor that affects both borrower APR and original loan amount.
  • Customers belonging to the low end of credit rating scale ('HR', 'E', 'D') got the most expensive loans at the smallest sums on average.
  • At the same loan amount less than 3k, low credit rating customers have loans at the interest rate varying between 20% and around 45% while high credit rating customers can get loans mostly below 25%.
  • Only customers with credit rating 'C' or above 'C' have access to the loan amount greater than or equal to 18k.
  • For the loan amounts higher than 24k, only customers with credit rating 'A' and 'AA' can get their loans approved.
