Project: Communicating Data Findings¶

Part II - Influences of Loan Properties on Their Borrower APRs¶

by Dorothy Nguyen¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I would like to discover what are the major loan features for predicting the borrower interest rate of loans. The main focus was on the amount of loan, monthly loan payment, and the characteristics of the borrowers such as their incomes and credit ratings.

Dataset Overview¶

  • The studied data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan provided by Prosper Marketplace, California-based pioneering company in the peer-to-peer lending industry in the U.S.
  • Since its establishment in 2005, Prosper has helped more than 1.3 million customers get access to over 21 billion USD in affordable loans (source).
  • Out of 81 columns, I selected about 15 columns that are relevant to this loan exploration project.
  • In the preliminary wrangling, I transformed some variables into their proper data types, for example, Income Range and Prosper Rating (Alpha) being converted to be ordinal types since they have intrinsic ranking order in their natures.

Distribution of Borrwer APR¶

Borrower APR in the dataset is approximately normally distrubuted with the peak around 20 percent. The first peak trails off till the next peaks at 30 and 35 percent.

Distributions of Loan Original Amount¶

  • Loan original amount takes on a wide range of values, from 1k at the lowest, to 35k at the highest and exhibits a strong right-skewed pattern.
  • Under the log transformation, the distribution of original loan amount takes on a multimodal shape, with the highest frequency between 3K and 5K, then quickly trailing off until the next spikes at 10k and 15k

Distribution of Monthly Loan Payment¶

  • Monthly loan payment takes value range between 0 and slightly above two thousands, and is positive skewed.
  • Plotted on the transformation scale, the data looks normally distributed, with one peak be approximately close to 200.

Distribution of categorical variables¶

  • In regards to loan status, 1/3 of loan agreements were completed sucessfully. The number of loans that fell into three different stages of defaulting on the loans (in the order of Past Due, Chargedoff, and Defaulted) were roughly half the amount of sucessful deals.
  • Prosper ratings are labeled AA, A, B, C, D, E, and HR, with AA being related to the lowest-risk customers. A large proportion of borrowers taking on loans are of Prosper rating C.
  • The income ranges of the customers is generally above average, with most of them in the 25k-50k or 50k-75k scale.

Borrower APR and Loan Original Amount Distribution by Two Ordinal Variables¶

  • Prosper rating score appears to have the strongest relationships with the two numeric variables but in two opposite directions, negative correlation with interest rate but positive relationship with Loan original amount.
  • Similarly, the customers with the higher income ranges seem to get the cheaper borrowing rate, and also tend to be provided with bigger on loan amount.

Borrower APR vs Loan Original Amount¶

  • Plotting Borrower APR and loan original amount on a logarithmic scale produces a negative relationship, meaning bigger quantities of loan resulting in cheaper borrowing rates for customers.
  • One of the reasonably explanatory factor is the fact that customers approved bigger amount of loan are also associated with cheaper loans due to their trustworthy credit scores or high income status. This worths further investigating in the upcoming multivariate exploration.

Borrower APR vs Loan Original Amount across Prosper Rating¶

  • Prosper rating scale appears to be the factor that affects both borrower APR and original loan amount.
  • Customers belonging to the low end of credit rating scale ('HR', 'E', 'D') got the most expensive loans at the smallest sums on average.
  • At the same loan amount less than 3k, low credit rating customers have loans at the interest rate varying between 20% and around 45% while high credit rating customers can get loans mostly below 25%.
  • Only customers with credit rating 'C' or above 'C' have access to the loan amount greater than or equal to 18k.
  • For the loan amounts higher than 24k, only customers with credit rating 'A' and 'AA' can get their loans approved.

Borrower APR vs Loan Original Amount across Monthly Loan Payment¶

  • The scatter plot depicts a significant postive correlation between loan original amount and monthly loan payment.
  • High proportion of borrowers seem to take loans smaller than 10k,mostly between 3K and 5K and pay less than 200 monthly.
  • There are few customers borrowing more than 30k and paying monthly more than 1k.

Borrower APR and Income Range accross Loan Status¶

  • Across all level of incomes, except for income range of 0, past due, chargedoff, and defaulted loans generally have higher interest rates.
  • On the contrary, completed loans seem to be the ones that have the lowest interest rate in all income ranges.
  • The large error bars for income staying at 0 are due to there being few loans for this income range customer, which consists only 621 loans in this data set. There are also few loans being cancelled or being in their progesses of final payment.
